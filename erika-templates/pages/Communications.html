Office Hours

Office hours offer you the opportunity to ask individual questions about the course material that you may have not had time to ask in class.

Office hours are held on a first-come-first-served drop-in basis with no appointment necessary. Sometimes office hours become increasingly busy the closer it is to a deadline. If students are waiting, discussions will be limited to ~10 minutes/student. Plan your use of office hours accordingly.

General Questions or Discussions

If you have general questions about the course or an assignment that are not covered in these course documents, please post your questions to a public channel in Slack so all students can benefit from the answer. If, as a student, you can answer a question posed, I encourage you to reply to your classmate.

Personal Topics

If you have a question about your particular assignment, grade, etc, please contact me privately.

All official communications should be done using RIT accounts (email or Slack). Furthermore, faculty may not be able to respond to requests that do not originate from an RIT account.

I check email and Slack frequently, but Slack DMs are usually the fastest way to reach me. If you do not receive a response from me within 24 hours, Monday-Friday, contact me again as it is likely I missed your first message. Messages over the weekend may take slightly longer for a response.

When using email, please make sure to use only the email address noted above. I do have others associated with RIT, but I do NOT check them! Always include a subject line and be sure to mention the class name and section. It’s also expected that you use complete sentences and punctuation, as well as sign your name.

myCourses Announcements

I will provide any course announcements through the Announcements on the Course Home Page. This includes any updates or changes to assignments or other course assignments, as well as general comments about assignment results or discussions. Please check your RIT e-mail & myCourses daily for important notifications.

Slack

Slack is NOT a replacement for attending class, studying, doing assignments, etc!

Slack is a way to extend our time together and build community in a more casual way - especially since we cannot hang out after class, in my office, etc.

You can contact me through email if you would like to discuss something privately, but for quick general discussions, I would prefer you use Slack that way everyone can stay connected. Within the Slack workspace, all RIT rules still apply and we reserve the right to remove anyone at any time for any reason.

Some rules:

  • Do NOT do someone's work for them (this ruins the learning process).
  • Do NOT explain exactly how to solve a problem (this ruins the learning process).
  • Do NOT use any discriminatory language. You will be banned from the server for that and may face other academic punishments. I have ZERO tolerance for discrimination.
  • Do NOT share large sections of code. Posts such as “Please debug this” and “Here’s my solution” will be considered a violation of the academic integrity policy.
  • DO ask yourself if your message is respectful. If it's disrespectful against any individual or group, you will see consequences.
  • DO ask questions about concepts, homework clarifications, etc.
  • DO share links or examples that seem useful.
  • DO have fun. You can do a lot of fun stuff in this course if you're willing to try and this is a great opportunity to get to know your classmates!

Name & Pronouns

Many people go by a name in daily life that is different from their legal name. In this course, we seek to address everyone in the manner that best respects their identity. You are invited (publicly or privately) to share the name and pronouns you wish us to use.

Additionally, you can:

  • Set a "nickname" in myCourses  by clicking on your name in the top right corner and selecting "Profile"
  • Change your "preferred name" in other RIT systems by going to https://start.rit.edu/ --> Directory Information --> Name Preferences Page

If you would like your name changed in legal documents (transcripts, etc.), complete this form and return it to the Registrar's Office.
