Name & Pronouns

Many people go by a name in daily life that is different from their legal name. In this course, we seek to address everyone in the manner that best respects their identity. You are invited (publicly or privately) to share the name and pronouns you wish us to use and when/how we should use them.

For example, while some professors will want you to use "Professor" or "Dr." with their last name, Prof. Mesh is comfortable with you all using her first name, "Erika", in casual conversations. In written correspondence, please use "Prof. Mesh" and the pronouns she/her when referring to her (i.e. emails, course evals, etc.).

Additionally, you can change your "preferred name" in other RIT systems by going to the https://start.rit.edu/ --> Directory Information --> Name Preferences Page.

Note that changing your Preferred Name will change your display name on some mail that goes to home addresses as well as RIT Email, RIT Directories, myCourses, Zoom, Early Alert, SIS, and other campus systems with data feeds from SIS. Only change your Preferred Name if you would like to change it for ALL of these RIT uses.

If you would like your name changed in legal documents (transcripts, etc.), complete this form and return it to the Registrar's Office.

During Class

Due to time constraints, it is important that you join class each day prepared to work. There will be times when we will discuss topics and issues in the form of a lecture. During this time in the class, you will be expected to take notes about the material being presented. I highly recommend taking hand-written notes during class. During the lecture time, you are also encouraged to ask questions about the topics being discussed or the supplemental readings you have completed.

Missing Class

In the case of an illness or unexpected absence, the student should try to communicate with the faculty in as timely of a manner as possible. When class is missed, it is up to the student to make up work and to acquire any course materials. Absences in any form do not postpone due dates unless agreed upon by the instructor.

Class Cancellation

If the faculty cannot attend a class session, students will be contacted via email and cancellation signs will be placed on classroom doors. Explicit instructions for cancellation will be communicated via myCourses and your RIT e-mail.

Outside of Class

myCourses Announcements

I will provide any course announcements through the Announcements on the Course Home Page. This includes any updates or changes to assignments or other course assignments, as well as general comments about assignment results or discussions. Please check your RIT e-mail & myCourses daily for important notifications.

Slack

Slack is a way to extend our time together and build community in a more casual way. People can have discussions or ask questions. Questions may be answered by myself, our TAs, or other students. 

Slack is NOT a replacement for attending class, studying, doing assignments, etc!

If you have general questions about the course or an assignment, please post your questions to a public channel in Slack so all students can benefit from the answer. If, as a student, you can answer a question posed, I encourage you to reply to your classmate.

Private Communications

If you have a question about your particular assignment, grade, etc, please contact me privately.

Slack DMs

I check email often, but Slack DMs are usually the fastest way to reach me. If you do not receive a response from me within 24 hours, Monday-Friday, contact me again as it is likely I missed your first message. Messages over the weekend may take slightly longer for a response. Unless a topic is sensitive, I strongly encourage you to copy the TAs on any messages to me by creating a group DM. The TAs can answer most questions and, regardless, I like them to be "in the loop" as much as possible.

Office Hours

Office hours offer you the opportunity to ask individual questions about the course material that you may have not had time to ask in class. Office hours are held on a first-come-first-served drop-in basis with no appointment necessary. Sometimes office hours become increasingly busy the closer it is to a deadline or the end of the semester. If students are waiting, discussions will be limited to ~10 minutes/student. Plan your use of office hours accordingly.
