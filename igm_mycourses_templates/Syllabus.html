Course Format

All core content, assignments, submissions, etc. will be made available to you at the beginning of each week according to the Course Schedule. We will use the scheduled class time for lecture, open discussions, team meetings, and 1on1 meetings as needed. Your consistent participation in these activities and the team project is required!

Each topic in this course will be supported via the required textbook and/or supplemental readings and have associated individual and project activities. In addition to class time, you should plan to spend a minimum of 8 hours weekly reading the provided resources and working on the individual and team assignments.

Please be sure to keep current with the readings for the course! While you are not expected to memorize/understand every detail of the readings, it is important that you read the materials so that you can participate in discussions and in-class activities. As you work through each week's material, remember that you’re here to learn something new, or to get better at something you already have experience with. I’m here to help you do that. Please don’t be afraid to ask for help when you need it. (I do actually enjoy helping you!)

If class needs to be cancelled for any reason, students will be contacted via email, and an announcement will be posted in myCourses and Slack.

Communications Guidelines

All official communications should be done using RIT accounts (email or Slack).
Faculty may not be able to respond to requests that do not originate from an RIT account.

myCourses Announcements

I will provide any course announcements through the Announcements on the Course Home Page. This includes any updates or changes to assignments or other course assignments, as well as general comments about assignment results or discussions. Please check your RIT e-mail & myCourses daily for important notifications.

Slack

Slack is a way to extend our time together and build community in a more casual way. People can have discussions or ask questions. Questions may be answered by myself, our TAs, or other students. 

Slack is NOT a replacement for attending class, studying, doing assignments, etc!

If you have general questions about the course or an assignment, please post your questions to a public channel in Slack so all students can benefit from the answer. If, as a student, you can answer a question posed, I encourage you to reply to your classmate.

Private Communications

If you have a question about your particular assignment, grade, etc, please contact me privately.

Slack DMs

I check email often, but Slack DMs are usually the fastest way to reach me. If you do not receive a response from me within 24 hours, Monday-Friday, contact me again as it is likely I missed your first message. Messages over the weekend may take slightly longer for a response. Unless a topic is sensitive, I strongly encourage you to copy the TAs on any messages to me by creating a group DM. The TAs can answer most questions and, regardless, I like them to be "in the loop" as much as possible.

Email

Always include a subject line and be sure to mention the class name and section. It’s also expected that you use complete sentences and punctuation, as well as sign your name.

Office Hours

Office hours offer you the opportunity to ask individual questions about the course material that you may have not had time to ask in class. Office hours are held on a first-come-first-served drop-in basis with no appointment necessary. Sometimes office hours become increasingly busy the closer it is to a deadline or the end of the semester. If students are waiting, discussions will be limited to ~10 minutes/student. Plan your use of office hours accordingly. 

Attendance & Participation

Due to time constraints, it is important that you join class each day prepared to work. There will be times when we will discuss topics and issues in the form of a lecture. During this time in the class, you will be expected to take notes about the material being presented. I highly recommend taking hand-written notes during class. During the lecture time, you are also encouraged to ask questions about the topics being discussed or the supplemental readings you have completed.

Course Absence Requests

In the case of an illness or unexpected absence, the student should try to communicate with the faculty in as timely of a manner as possible. When class is missed, it is up to the student to make up work and to acquire any course materials. Absences in any form do not postpone due dates unless agreed upon by the instructor.

Zoom

Zoom is available for use only when absolutely needed to support periodic guests or students who need to be temporarily remote due to circumstances outside their control. "I overslept" or "Don't feel like walking to class in the cold" are not sufficient reasons to use Zoom, but I am happy to open Zoom if you are ill, think you may be at risk of spreading an illness (whether or not RIT has officially told you to quarantine), or have some other extenuating circumstances.

I know it's not ideal, but it's MUCH better than missing class and trying to keep up using just the lecture slides. The camera in the room won't show the whiteboards, which I use a lot, but I'll do my best to take pictures to post in Slack later.

If you need to participate remotely, please contact me with as much advance notice as possible. You will only be able to access Zoom via an RIT account & will only be admitted from the waiting room if you've pre-arranged with me to listen in remotely. (I.e. I won't be opening Zoom unless I'm already expecting someone.)

Food and Drink

Food and drink are not permitted in the computer labs.  Food and drink have already been prohibited for years before covid due to the nature of spilling liquids on expensive lab computers (yes, it has happened!) or using greasy fingers on keyboards (eww).   

The ONLY exception is: a beverage in a closed, spill-proof container with a straw or spout.  A strong, structured bottle that contains the beverage and a sturdy straw will work.  If you are a hot beverage drinker, keep an insulated tumbler with you.  No, a paper cup with a plastic straw from Salsarita's or Midnight Oil does not count.

Student Support

You’re here to learn something new, or to get better at something you already have experience with. We're here to help you do that.

Success in this course depends heavily on your personal health and wellbeing. The academic demands in this course and your other classes can be understandably difficult. It is normal to feel anxious about your academic ability, especially when unexpected life events emerge.

Your other instructors and I strongly encourage you to reframe challenges as an unavoidable pathway to success. Reflect on your role in taking care of yourself throughout the term, before the demands of exams and projects reach their peak. Please feel free to reach out to me about any difficulty you may be having that may impact your performance in this course as soon as it occurs and before it becomes unmanageable.

RIT's Academic Alert System

This course participates in the RIT Starfish academic alert system, which is designed to promote student success through communication between students, instructors, and advisors. When I am concerned about a student’s academic performance, I may raise an academic alert to notify the student as well as their advisor(s).  If you receive an academic alert email, it is your responsibility to contact me as soon as possible to discuss the issue, its potential impact on your success in this course, and identify resources to help move you forward. For more information about the Starfish system, visit www.rit.edu/starfish.
